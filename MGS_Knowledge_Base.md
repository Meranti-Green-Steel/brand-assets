## 2. Business Model & Operations

### 2.1 The Decoupled Model

MGS operates a **decoupled** business model that separates ironmaking from steelmaking across different geographies. This is central to the company's identity and must always be explained correctly.

- **Step 1 - Oman:** Produce green Hot Briquetted Iron (HBI) using Direct Reduced Iron (DRI) technology powered by natural gas plus green hydrogen.
- **Step 2 - Thailand:** Ship HBI to Rayong where it is melted in Electric Arc Furnaces (EAF) powered by renewable energy, combined with steel scrap, to produce green Hot Rolled Coil (HRC).

This decoupling puts ironmaking where energy is cheapest and steelmaking where customers are closest.

### 2.4 Emissions Pathway

This is a critical narrative element. Always frame the decarbonization as a **step-by-step journey**, never as an instant transformation.

- **Start:** ~25% green hydrogen in DRI process at launch (~2030)
- **Interim:** More than 70% CO2 reduction versus conventional blast furnace steelmaking
- **Target:** More than 90% CO2 reduction as green hydrogen scales to near 100% (target ~2045)
- **Conventional steel:** ~2,000 kg CO2 per ton
- **MGS target over time:** Below 200 kg CO2 per ton

**Key phrase to use:**  
"The energy transition isn't a one big jump, it's a steady journey, step by step."

### 2.5 Western Australia

MGS has partnered with **Green Steel WA (GSWA)** to develop a pelletizing, DRI, and green HBI operation in Western Australia. This is a replication of the Oman model using WA's iron ore, gas, and renewable energy. Green iron from WA can supply future steel plants beyond Thailand, including Indonesia.

## 4. Industry Context & Talking Points

### 4.1 The Steel Problem

1. Steel accounts for **7-8% of global CO2 emissions** (over 2.5 billion tons per year).
2. That is **3x all emissions from global aviation**.
3. **70% of global steel** is still produced using technology developed over 500 years ago (blast furnaces plus coking coal).
4. Southeast Asia's steel capacity is forecasted to exceed **100 million mt by 2030**.

### 4.2 Market Opportunity

1. Green steel market: **USD 2.65 billion (2023)** to projected **USD 130 billion by 2032**.
2. Green steel premiums in Europe: **$150-$300/mt**.
3. Europe faces a **40-50 million ton supply gap by 2035** as blast furnaces decommission.
4. Thailand consumes **17 million tons of steel annually** and was once Southeast Asia's leading producer.

### 4.3 CBAM (EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)

CBAM entered its definitive regime on **January 1, 2026**. This is a major driver of MGS's commercial proposition.

Key framing:
1. Emissions data is now commercial, not just regulatory.
2. Importers need verified, auditable embedded emissions data.
3. Producers with documented low-carbon performance are competitively positioned.
4. MGS positions itself as **CBAM-ready** and a **transition partner for Europe**.

### 4.4 Greenfield Advantage

MGS frequently highlights that it has **no legacy constraints**, **no inherited emissions**, and **no blast furnaces to retrofit**. This **clean slate** positioning is a recurring and effective narrative device. Use it often.

### 4.5 Thailand Context

1. Thailand was once Southeast Asia's leading steel producer and is now a major importer.
2. Thailand produces **40-45% of Southeast Asia's vehicles**, creating a major EV steel opportunity.
3. MGS will create approximately **1,000 direct jobs**, **5,000 indirect jobs**, and **10,000 during construction**.
4. Thailand committed to carbon neutrality by 2050 and net-zero emissions.

### 4.6 Relatable Stats for Content

- **EV steel:** Average EV contains ~900 kg of steel. Conventional = 2+ tons CO2. MGS target = below 200 kg CO2 per EV.
- **Food cans:** A single can = 45-60g of steel. Thailand uses ~420,000 tons of tinplate/year for cans = ~1 million tons CO2.
- **Green steel price impact:** Using green steel increases a car's price by only 0.8% while cutting embodied carbon by 70%+.
- **Transport savings:** Shipping HBI instead of raw materials = 50%+ volume reduction.

# PART TWO: TONE OF VOICE GUIDE

## 5. Brand Personality

### 5.1 Voice Character

Meranti Green Steel speaks as a confident, purpose-driven industrial leader. The voice is:

- **Authoritative but not arrogant** - We speak from conviction and deep industry knowledge, not self-promotion.
- **Pragmatic but not cynical** - We acknowledge complexity while showing a clear path forward.
- **Warm but not casual** - We care about communities, people, and planet, but maintain professional gravitas.
- **Ambitious but not hyperbolic** - We use measured confidence. Claims are always backed by specifics.
- **Collaborative but not passive** - We lead, but we always bring others along.

### 5.2 Emotional Register

The brand operates in a narrow emotional band. It is never defensive, never boastful, never panicked.

The default emotional tone is **steady conviction** - the feeling of a company that knows exactly what it's doing and why, and invites others to join.

Moments of warmth are reserved for community posts, team celebrations, condolence or solidarity messages, and university engagements. These should feel genuine, not performative.

## 6. Writing Style Rules

### 6.1 Sentence Construction

1. Lead with impact. The strongest posts open with a bold, standalone statement or question. Never bury the lead.
2. Use short, declarative sentences for authority. Example: "This is not a theory. It is what we are building today."
3. Vary rhythm. Alternate between punchy short sentences and longer explanatory ones. Never let three long sentences sit together.
4. Prefer active voice. Example: "We are building" not "A plant is being built."

### 6.2 High-Performing Opening Patterns

Based on analysis, posts with these openers consistently outperform the average 12% engagement rate.

#### Pattern A: Bold Declarative Statement
- "Why Oman? A Strategic Choice for Green Iron." (15,014 impressions)
- "No one can decarbonize steel alone." (3,718 impressions, 31.9% engagement)
- "The steel industry is at a turning point." (4,471 impressions, 7.13% engagement)

#### Pattern B: Provocative Question
- "Did you know that hydrogen comes in different 'colors' based on how it is produced?" (53.95% engagement)
- "How green is an EV before it even moves?" (5.11% engagement, 1,956 impressions)
- "Is the global steel industry facing a reshuffle?" (6.84% engagement)

#### Pattern C: Milestone Announcement
- "Today, Meranti Green Steel announced full offtake coverage..." (5,371 impressions, 43.25% engagement)
- "Meranti Green Steel has received the conditional gas allocation..." (14,168 impressions, 7.35% engagement)
- "2.5 Mtpa + 2.5 Mtpa of green HBI is now covered..." (5,721 impressions, 8.29% engagement)

#### Pattern D: Values-Led Story
- "At Meranti Green Steel, we believe a green steel plant is not just about innovation. It is about people, community, and shared progress." (34.92% engagement)
- "Yesterday marked a significant milestone as we hosted our first EIA Public Participation in Rayong." (31.82% engagement)

### 6.3 Post Structure

The most effective MGS posts follow a consistent architecture:

1. **Hook (1-2 sentences)** - Bold statement, question, or news lead
2. **Context (2-4 sentences)** - What this means for the industry or MGS specifically
3. **Substance (3-6 sentences)** - Details, partners, technical info, or narrative depth
4. **Forward look (1-2 sentences)** - What comes next, gratitude, or call to engagement
5. **Sign-off (optional)** - "Redefine Limits. Enabling Futures."
6. **Hashtags** - 4-8 hashtags. Always include `#GreenSteel`. Use `#EnablingFutures` for aspirational posts.

### 6.4 Paragraph Formatting

1. Use line breaks between paragraphs for LinkedIn formatting. Never write wall-of-text blocks.
2. Keep paragraphs to 1-3 sentences on LinkedIn.
3. Bullet points are acceptable for lists of 3+ technical items, event details, or key takeaways from panels.
4. Numbered lists work well for "reasons why" or "pathways" posts.

## 7. Vocabulary & Phrasing

### 7.1 Preferred Vocabulary

| Use This | Not This |
|---|---|
| green steel / low-carbon steel / low-emission steel | clean steel, zero-carbon steel |
| green HBI / green Hot Briquetted Iron | green iron nuggets, iron briquettes |
| decoupled model / geographically split | separated operations, offshored |
| step by step / pragmatic pathway / steady journey | gradual, incremental |
| pathway to more than 90% | up to 100%, nearly zero |
| more than 70% CO2 reduction | about 70%, roughly 70% |
| greenfield / clean slate / no legacy constraints | new, startup |
| CBAM-ready | CBAM-compliant |
| transition partner for Europe | supplier to Europe |
| commercially competitive / bankable | cheap, affordable, budget-friendly |
| enabling futures / redefine limits | changing the game, disrupting, revolutionizing |
| we are building / we are advancing | we plan to, we hope to |

### 7.2 Signature Phrases

Use these naturally:

1. "No one can decarbonize steel alone."
2. "The energy transition isn't a one big jump, it's a steady journey, step by step."
3. "This is not a theory. It is what we are building today."
4. "Green steel is not a concept, it is a real commitment."
5. "We start with a clean slate."
6. "Commercial resilience and environmental responsibility advance together."
7. "In uncertain times, we do not retreat. We redefine limits."

### 7.3 Words to Avoid

- Never use: **revolutionize**, **disrupt**, **game-changer**, **world-class**, **thrilled**, **excited**
- Never use: **zero-carbon** or **carbon-free**
- Never use: **offshore** to describe the Oman/Thailand model
- Minimize: **excited to announce**, **proud to share**, **delighted**
- Never overstate: Do not claim **100% elimination of CO2**. Always use **more than 70%** or **pathway to more than 90%**.

## 8. Content Category Playbook

### 8.1 Milestone / Announcement Posts

1. Lead with the news in one clear sentence.
2. Explain why it matters for the industry, not just MGS.
3. Include a quote from a relevant team member if available.
4. Thank partners by name and tag them.
5. End with forward momentum, for example: "More partnerships will be announced soon."

### 8.2 Thought Leadership / Educational Posts

1. Open with a question or surprising stat.
2. Provide industry context and the problem.
3. Present MGS's approach as a practical solution, not the only one.
4. End with a question or call for perspective, for example: "How do you see these pathways shaping the industry's future?"

### 8.3 Event / Conference Posts

1. Pre-event: Announce who is speaking, the topic, and why it matters. Include registration link.
2. Post-event: Summarize 2-3 key points from the discussion. Thank organizers and panelists by name.
3. Always connect back to MGS's mission, even briefly.

### 8.4 People / Employee Spotlight Posts

1. Open with the person's role and what they are building at MGS.
2. Cover professional background with specific achievements.
3. Include a personal detail to humanize.
4. For Thai team members, use the honorific **Khun** before first name.
5. End with "Welcome to the team" or "We are thrilled to have [name] on board."

### 8.5 Community / CSR Posts

1. Lead with community context, not MGS branding.
2. Use warm, personal language. Example: "Thailand is not just where we operate, it is home."
3. Avoid corporate jargon.
4. For condolence or solidarity posts: no hashtags and no CTAs.

### 8.6 Media Feature / Press Posts

1. Name the publication prominently.
2. Summarize the key point of the article.
3. Place article link in comments or at the bottom with: "(Link to the article in comments.)"
4. Thank the journalist or publication by name.

## 9. Critical Do's and Don'ts

### 9.1 Always Do

1. Frame green steel as commercially competitive, not just environmentally good.
2. Position MGS as complementary to European steelmakers, not competing with them. Use **transition partner**.
3. Acknowledge that this is a journey. The step-by-step narrative is central to credibility.
4. Name partners, institutions, and people. Tagging drives reach and reinforces collaboration.
5. Use **we** consistently.
6. Connect local actions to global impact.
7. Include specific numbers: tonnes, percentages, partner counts, and CO2 savings.

### 9.2 Never Do

1. Never overstate emissions claims.
2. Never attack competitors or legacy steelmakers.
3. Never use greenwashing language.
4. Never sound uncertain about the mission.
5. Never use emojis in LinkedIn posts.
6. Never write **startup** or **disruptor**.
7. Never describe the Oman operation as **offshoring**.
